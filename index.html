Natural Overview

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia occupies the largest portion of the Arabian Peninsula. It consists of narrow coastal plains along the Red Sea (Tihama Plains), followed eastward by mountain ranges that extend along the country (the Hijaz and Asir Mountains, rising over 2,000 meters). Further inland, vast deserts and rocky plateaus dominate the central region, covering nearly 90% of the total area. The largest of these are the An Nafud Desert in the north and the Rub' al Khali (Empty Quarter) in the south. In the east, along the Arabian Gulf coast, wide coastal plains stretch across the region.

Area

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia occupies nearly four-fifths of the Arabian Peninsula, with a total area of more than 2,250,000 square kilometers.

National Day of the Kingdom

Saudi Arabia celebrates its National Day annually on September 23, commemorating the historic unification of the Kingdom by King Abdulaziz bin Abdulrahman Al Saud, who officially proclaimed the establishment of the Kingdom in 1932 (1351 AH).
